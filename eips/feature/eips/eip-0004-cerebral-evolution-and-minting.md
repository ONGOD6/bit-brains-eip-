## Abstract

This EIP defines the creation, minting, and initial lifecycle of Cerebral tokens
within the Bit Brains protocol. Cerebrals represent the post-Genesis evolutionary
state of Brains and serve as the foundational entities for higher-order coordination,
staking, and intelligence within the system.

Cerebrals are implemented as non-fungible tokens and are designed to be forward-
compatible with future protocol stages, including the emergence of Autonomous
Intelligence Tokens (AITs).

---

## Motivation

The Brain stage establishes participation, alignment, and economic contribution
through Proof-of-Care and epoch-based rewards. However, Brains are intentionally
limited in expressiveness and long-term agency.

The Cerebral stage introduces a durable, evolvable entity capable of representing
persistent state, composability, and future autonomy. By separating Brain mechanics
from Cerebral evolution, the protocol enables:

- Clear lifecycle progression  
- Incentivized long-term alignment  
- Modular protocol upgrades  
- Governance-controlled emergence of intelligence  

---

## Cerebral Overview

A Cerebral is the evolutionary successor to a Genesis Brain.

Cerebrals are non-fungible protocol entities represented as ERC-721 tokens. Each
Cerebral corresponds to a specific Genesis Brain and reflects that Brainâ€™s behavior
and alignment during the Brain stage.

Key properties of Cerebrals include:

- Persistence across protocol epochs  
- Eligibility for extended staking and coordination  
- Forward compatibility with autonomous intelligence primitives  
- Governance-controlled evolution  

At the time of minting, Cerebrals do not possess autonomous intelligence, agency,
or self-directed behavior. Autonomy and intelligence emerge only through subsequent
protocol stages defined in future EIPs.
